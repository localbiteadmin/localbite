CITY RESEARCH NOTES
════════════════════════════════════════

Logroño is the capital of La Rioja — Spain's premier wine region. The food
scene is defined by pintxos culture (similar to but distinct from Basque
pintxos), exceptional Rioja wine, and produce from La Rioja's famous huerta.

KEY DISHES AND CULINARY VOCABULARY:
- Pintxos (also pinchos locally): small bites served at the bar. Unlike San
  Sebastián, many Logroño bars specialize in a SINGLE signature pintxo —
  ordering the right thing at each bar is part of the local culture.
- Bar Soriano champiñones: grilled mushrooms — the most famous pintxo in
  Logroño. Often called "the mushrooms of Soriano" by locals.
- Cojonudo: chorizo + quail egg on bread — a Logroño pintxo institution.
- Patatas a la riojana: potatoes with chorizo and piquillo peppers
- Caparrones: red kidney beans from Anguiano, often with chistorra
- Pimientos de piquillo de Lodosa: roasted red peppers, often stuffed
- Menestra de verduras riojana: seasonal vegetable stew
- Chistorra: thin Navarran/Riojan sausage
- Morcilla riojana: blood sausage — Ferrero Rocher de morcilla at Bar El Muro
- Trucha de La Rioja: river trout
- Espárragos de La Rioja: white asparagus
- Rioja wine: Tempranillo dominant, Garnacha, Viura for whites
- Zurito: small beer, the standard pintxos crawl drink
- Txikito: small glass of wine
- Poteo: the act of doing a pintxos crawl bar to bar

KEY NON-MICHELIN RESTAURANTS (likely to appear in sources):
- La Cocina de Ramón (Calle Portales, 30) — traditional Riojan, market-driven
- Tondeluna — contemporary Riojan, run by Francis Paniego protégé
- La Chispa Adecuada — creative modern Riojan
- Juan Carlos Ferrando — traditional upscale
