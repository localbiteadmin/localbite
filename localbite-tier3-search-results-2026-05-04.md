# LocalBite — Tier 3 Search Results: Named Critics at Regional Publications
*Run: 2026-05-04. Tier 3 = publications identified in candidate list where named critic was unknown.*

---

## The Structural Finding

The dominant result across all Tier 3 searches — Italy and Spain — is that regional newspapers generally **do not have easily-findable dedicated named food critics**. Web search consistently returns:
- The newspaper's homepage or Wikipedia page
- General gastronomia award/event news
- National-level critics who are not city-specific

This is not a methodology failure. It reflects a genuine structural reality: city-level restaurant criticism in Italy and Spain is produced primarily by:
1. Independent food sites and blogs (Cronache di Gusto, Complicidad Gastronómica, Observación Gastronómica)
2. Specialized national publications with regional editions (Gambero Rosso, CiboToday, Scatti di Gusto)
3. A small number of named regional newspaper critics who are nationally recognized (Maribona/ABC, Visintin/CdS, Campos/La Voz)

The "systematic gap" of no Italian regional newspaper in the LocalBite fleet may accurately reflect the Italian food journalism landscape rather than a pipeline failure.

## The Main Insight This Session Produced

The hypothesis that "Italian regional newspapers are a systematic gap" is only partially correct. The newspapers exist and cover food. But:

1. Most do not have a **dedicated named food critic** with bylined restaurant reviews in the editorial prose style LocalBite requires.
2. The ones that do (La Stampa/Massobrio, ABC/Maribona) are already in the candidate list and were found in Phase A country-level searches — not in Tier 3 regional searches.
3. The real local food critic niche in Italian cities is filled by independent food sites (Cronache di Gusto in Palermo), specialized national publications with regional contributors (Scatti di Gusto, CiboToday), and guide-affiliated writers (Gambero Rosso regional correspondents).

**For the pipeline:** This means the pre-research template Search 8 ("city newspaper food critic named") will have a lower yield than expected for Italian cities. For Italian cities, Search 8 should target both city newspapers AND local food sites/blogs. Spain is similar but Tapas Magazine's top 35 list is a reliable source for finding named regional critics (it surfaced Campos for Galicia and Belber for Málaga).
