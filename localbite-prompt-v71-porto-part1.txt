# ── NEIGHBOURHOOD STRUCTURE ──────────────────────────────────
# Porto's food scene clusters in the historic centre (Ribeira, Miragaia),
# the creative neighbourhood of Bonfim, and the traditional Cedofeita/Massarelos.
# Foz do Douro is the upscale waterfront area further west.
# Matosinhos (adjacent municipality) is the famous seafood destination and
# is commonly covered alongside Porto by food writers — include.
# Gaia (Vila Nova de Gaia) is across the Douro and technically a separate
# municipality but is covered by Porto food writers — include cautiously.

TIER_1_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Ribeira, Bonfim, Cedofeita, Miragaia
TIER_2_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Foz do Douro, Massarelos, Lordelo do Ouro, Campanhã
TIER_3_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Matosinhos (adjacent), Gaia (across river)

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_INCLUDE:
  Ribeira, Bonfim, Cedofeita, Miragaia, Foz do Douro, Massarelos,
  Lordelo do Ouro, Campanhã, Paranhos, Ramalde,
  Matosinhos (if covered by Porto food writers as part of Porto food scene),
  Vila Nova de Gaia waterfront (if covered as part of Porto food scene)

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_EXCLUDE:
  Braga (separate city 50km north), Guimarães (separate city),
  Póvoa de Varzim, Vila do Conde, Espinho (all separate municipalities)
