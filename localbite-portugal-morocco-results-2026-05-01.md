**Porto (17R, 0→2 BP)**

**This is the critical diagnostic result.** Porto has 3 EN + 1 PT (Visão/Florbela Alves). The reason for 0BP despite having a PT source is the **Turin pattern**: Visão covers entirely different Porto restaurants than Portoalities, Go Ask A Local, and Culinary Backstreets. Under the new definition: Terra Nova and Casa Expresso both have EN+EN pairs from different publishers (e.g., Portoalities vs Go Ask A Local, or Portoalities vs Culinary Backstreets) → both become BP. Porto goes from 0%→12%.

Phase B: No additional PT press sources found for Porto that would overlap with the EN-covered restaurants. Público/Fugas 451 blocked. No Observador article URLs found for Porto pack restaurants.

Phase C: Terra Nova and Casa Expresso — no qualifying PT-language journalist reviews found. These restaurants are covered only by EN travel blogs.

**Porto structural note:** Porto at 0BP with a PT source present is explained by source-restaurant coverage divergence, not a source quality problem. The definition change is the complete fix. Beyond that, adding a second PT source (if one could be found) that overlaps with the EN-covered restaurants would create immediate BP under the current definition — but no such source was found.

## Structural Patterns — Morocco vs Italy Comparison

**Morocco is the definition-change story, not the new-source story.**

Italy had 3 verified new sources (Puntarella Rossa for Rome, The Infatuation for Venice, Scatti di Gusto for Trieste) that create immediate BP under the *current* definition. Portugal/Morocco has zero such finds.

The reason is structural: 
- Italy's non-BP restaurants lack EN+IT crosses — adding an IT source to an EN-heavy restaurant creates immediate BP
- Portugal's non-BP restaurants already have PT sources in the pack, but they cover *different* restaurants
- Morocco's non-BP restaurants already have FR sources in the pack, but they cover *different* restaurants

In both Portugal and Morocco, the cross-language sources exist — they just don't overlap with the right restaurants. The definition change removes the language requirement and unlocks the BP for all publisher-independent pairs, regardless of whether the sources happen to cover the same restaurants in the same language.
