⚠ THIN CITY RISK: Catania has limited English-language food media
compared to Palermo. May approach ≥10R threshold margin. Monitor
restaurant count during Phase 2.

Italian-language sources: Phase 1 should search for:
  "migliori ristoranti Catania osterie trattorie 2024 2025"
  "dove mangiare Catania guida gastronomica 2025"
  "Catania ristoranti consigliati critico gastronomico 2025"
  Named IT Catania/Sicily food writers and bloggers

Catanese food identity: Catania has a proud, distinctive street
food culture different from Palermo. The pipeline should target:
  - Arancine/arancini (rice balls — the spelling debate is local
    culture; Catania uses masculine arancino)
  - Granita con brioche (Catania's defining breakfast)
  - Pasta alla Norma (eggplant, tomato, ricotta salata —
    invented in Catania, named after Bellini's opera)
  - Pesce spada (swordfish) from the Pescheria fish market
  - Horsemeat (cavallo) — a Catania specialty
  - Rosticceria (fried street food shops)

Taormina contamination: Taormina is heavily marketed alongside
Catania (same province, ~50km north) and has 5 Michelin-starred
restaurants. All are outside the city bounding box. Any restaurant
described as "in Taormina", "overlooking the bay of Naxos",
or "on the Etna coast near Taormina" must be rejected.

Both-pool requirements: No Phase 0 sources confirmed. Phase 1
must find qualifying EN and IT sources. Different publishers,
different languages — qualifies for both-pool.
