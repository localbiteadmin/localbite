NEIGHBOURHOOD STRUCTURE
════════════════════════════════════════

Research basis: Valladolid has 12 official administrative districts
(purely electoral/census — not used socially or by food writers).
The relevant units are barrios (social neighbourhoods). Research
via valladolid.com/turismo, Diario de Valladolid, and local food
sources confirms the dining scene is heavily concentrated in the
historic centre, with secondary scenes in adjacent and residential
barrios. Generic neighbourhood names prefixed with city name per
naming convention to prevent centroid collisions.

TIER_1_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Valladolid Centro, Paseo de Zorrilla
TIER_2_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Valladolid Delicias, Caño Argales
TIER_3_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Rondilla, Valladolid La Victoria

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_INCLUDE:

Paseo de Zorrilla, Acera de Recoletos, Valladolid Delicias,
Las Delicias, Caño Argales, Rondilla, Valladolid La Victoria,
La Victoria, Huerta del Rey, Parquesol, Girón

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_EXCLUDE:
Salamanca, Burgos, León, Palencia, Segovia, Zamora, or any

being the city itself), or another Castilian city.
Also exclude: wineries and bodegas in the Ribera del Duero,
Rueda, or Cigales DO zones unless the restaurant is physically
located within Valladolid city limits.

LOCAL CONTEXT
════════════════════════════════════════

LOCAL_FOOD_VOCABULARY:
— lechazo (roast suckling lamb — the defining dish of Valladolid
  and Castilla y León; asadores specialising in lechazo al horno
  are the city's most distinctive restaurant category)
— torreznos (crispy pork belly — a Valladolid bar staple,
  frequently mentioned in local tapas writing)
— morcilla (blood sausage — local Castilian variety, distinct
  from Burgos morcilla)
— bacalao (salt cod — several institutions built around it,
  notably Don Bacalao which is a local landmark)
— verdejo (white wine grape — dominant in nearby Rueda DO,
  the default wine pairing at Valladolid restaurants)
— Ribera del Duero / Rueda / Cigales / Toro (the four wine DOs
  converging on Valladolid — highly relevant to restaurant coverage)
— pincho / pinchos (tapa format dominant in Valladolid bar culture)
— Pincho de Oro / El Pincho de Oro (the city's annual tapas
  competition — a major local food calendar event, use in
  awards queries to find local food writers covering it)
— Concurso Nacional de Pinchos y Tapas de Valladolid (the national
  competition hosted here annually — generates strong local press)
— Mercado del Val (19th-century market rehabilitated as a gastro
  market; a key location reference in local food writing)

Use 2–3 vocabulary queries in Spanish targeting lechazo, torreznos,
and Pincho de Oro to catch local-register food writing that
city-wide queries miss.
