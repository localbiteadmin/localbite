NEIGHBOURHOOD STRUCTURE
════════════════════════════════════════

Research basis: Official Santiago de Compostela municipal structure
verified via local sources. The Casco Histórico (UNESCO World Heritage
Site) is overwhelmingly the dominant dining area — the Cathedral,
Praza do Obradoiro, Rúa do Franco, Rúa da Raíña, and Mercado de
Abastos all fall within it. Secondary scenes exist in adjacent
neighbourhoods just outside the historic walls.

Note on naming: Generic names prefixed per project convention.
"Casco Histórico" and "Ensanche" appear in other fleet cities
and must be prefixed throughout the JSON output.

TIER_1_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Santiago Casco Histórico, San Pedro
TIER_2_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Santiago Ensanche, Agra do Orzán
TIER_3_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Fontiñas, Vista Alegre

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_INCLUDE:
Santiago Casco Histórico (Cathedral, Praza do Obradoiro,
Praza das Praterías, Rúa do Franco, Rúa da Raíña, Rúa Nova,
Mercado de Abastos, Rúa de San Pedro area), San Pedro (Barrio
de San Pedro, just outside historic walls), Santiago Ensanche
(modern expansion district), Agra do Orzán, Fontiñas, Vista Alegre

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_EXCLUDE:
A Coruña, Vigo, Pontevedra, Lugo, Ourense, or any location
described as "outside Santiago", "Galicia region", "nearby town",
"day trip from Santiago", or another Galician city or municipality.
The Rías Baixas wine region produces the Albariño served in Santiago
but restaurants in Cambados, O Grove, or other towns are excluded.

LOCAL CONTEXT
════════════════════════════════════════

LOCAL_FOOD_VOCABULARY:
— pulpo á feira (octopus with paprika, olive oil and cachelos —
  the defining Galician dish, essential search term)
— percebes (goose barnacles — hyper-local prestige seafood,
  Atlantic Galician coast exclusive)
— empanada gallega / empanada de millo (filled pastry with
  cornmeal crust — signals local food writing)
— lacón con grelos (cured pork shoulder with turnip greens —
  quintessential Galician winter dish)
— caldo gallego (broth with greens, beans, potatoes, chorizo)
— tarta de Santiago (almond cake with cross motif — city's
  signature dessert, DOP protected)
— Albariño (Rías Baixas DO white wine — the default wine pairing
  throughout Galician food writing)
— Ribeiro (DO white wine region, also common in Santiago)
— vieiras (large scallops — the pilgrim symbol of Santiago,
  frequently on menus)
— zamburiñas (small queen scallops — cheaper, common in tapas)
— zorza (seasoned raw pork mince — Galician cooking staple)
— Mercado de Abastos (Santiago's central market — essential
  local term, anchor of the contemporary dining scene)

GALICIAN LANGUAGE NOTE: Galicia has its own co-official language
(Galego/Gallego). Named food writers may write in Galician (GL),
Spanish (ES), or English (EN). GL + EN sources from different
publishers qualify as both-pool under the standard definition.
Search in all three languages. Galician vocabulary signals:
"restaurante", "gastronomía galega", "cociña galega", "marisco".

Use 2 Cross-6 vocabulary queries targeting pulpo á feira,
percebes, and Mercado de Abastos to catch local-register food
writing in both Spanish and Galician that generic queries miss.

SOURCE_EXAMPLES:
— Local Santiago/Galician press: El Correo Gallego (main daily),
  La Voz de Galicia (regional), Praza Pública (Galician language),
  Turismo de Santiago official guides
— Regional gastronomy: Guía Repsol Galicia section,
  El País Gastro (Galicia/northwest desk)

  Do not manufacture it if EN sources don't qualify.
