⚠ THIN CITY RISK: Lecce is a small city (~90,000 inhabitants)
with limited English-language food media. May fall below ≥10R
publish threshold. Run Lecce last within Batch F and review
restaurant count before committing to index.

Italian-language sources: Phase 1 should search for:
  "migliori ristoranti Lecce osterie trattorie 2024 2025"
  "dove mangiare Lecce guida gastronomica 2025"
  "Lecce ristoranti consigliati critico gastronomico 2025"
  Named IT Lecce/Salento food writers and bloggers

Salentine food identity: Lecce has a distinctive food culture
rooted in cucina povera (peasant cooking) elevated by quality
local produce. The pipeline should target:
  - Pasticciotti leccesi (custard-filled pastry — the defining
    sweet of Lecce, found at Pasticceria Natale and others)
  - Rustico leccese (puff pastry with tomato and mozzarella)
  - Ciceri e tria (pasta with fried pasta strips and chickpeas)
  - Frisella (dried bread soaked in olive oil and tomato)
  - Pittu (local dialect for polpette di pane)
  - Masserie (farmhouse restaurants) within city limits only
  - Natural wine bars in the centro storico

Salento contamination: The Salento peninsula is heavily promoted
as a single destination. Many food guides cover the entire region
under "Lecce" or "Salento." The pipeline must apply strict
bounding box discipline — only restaurants within the Comune
di Lecce qualify. Coastal towns (Otranto, Gallipoli, Porto
Cesareo, Santa Maria di Leuca) are separate municipalities
regardless of how travel writers group them.

Bros' note: Bros' (chef Floriano Pellegrino) was the most famous
Lecce restaurant internationally and lost its Michelin star in
2026. It may still have strong editorial coverage from 2023–2025.
Include if editorial prose quotes exist and the restaurant is
being recommended for food quality, not just for its former star.

Both-pool requirements: No Phase 0 sources confirmed. Phase 1
must find qualifying EN and IT sources. If fewer than 10R can
be reached from qualifying sources, park Lecce and document
