Italian-language sources: Rome has a rich IT-language food media
ecosystem. Phase 1 should actively search for:
  - Named Italian food critics covering Rome (cerca i giornalisti
    gastronomici a Roma)
  - Blogs written by Rome-based food writers in Italian
  - Italian food publications covering Rome restaurants
  - Dissapore, Gambero Rosso blog (with COI flag), Identità Golose

Both-pool requirements: To qualify for both-pool, need at least one
EN source AND one IT/other-language source from different publishers.
The two Katie Parla URLs above are from the same publisher (katieparla.com)
and count as ONE source for pool purposes. The pipeline must find at
least one independent IT-language source for both-pool to be achievable.

Cucina romana: Focus on traditional Roman dishes — carbonara, cacio e pepe,
amatriciana, coda alla vaccinara, carciofi alla giudia, supplì, pizza
al taglio, gelato. Restaurants serving these with independent editorial
endorsement are the target.

Neighbourhood focus: Do NOT over-concentrate on Centro Storico tourist
traps. Prioritise sources that recommend restaurants in Testaccio,
Trastevere, Pigneto, Ostiense, Tor Pignattara — where Romans actually eat.

Exclusion reminders:
  - All Michelin-starred restaurants (listed above) MUST be excluded
  - Tourist board content = reject
  - Aggregator content (TripAdvisor, Google, TheFork) = reject
  - PR-generated content = reject
  - Anonymous editorial team credits = reject (no named human author)
