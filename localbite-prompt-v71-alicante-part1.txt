NEIGHBOURHOOD STRUCTURE
════════════════════════════════════════

Research basis: Official Alicante municipal structure and dining
geography verified via local sources. Administrative districts are
not used by food writers or locals for navigation — social barrios
and named areas are the relevant unit. Dining scene is concentrated
in the historic centre and waterfront with secondary scenes in
Ensanche and the marina.

Note on naming: Generic names prefixed per project convention to
prevent centroid collisions with other fleet cities. "Centro",
"El Barrio", "Ensanche", and "El Puerto" all appear in other
cities and must be prefixed throughout the JSON output.

TIER_1_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Alicante Centro, Alicante El Barrio
TIER_2_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Alicante Ensanche, Alicante El Puerto
TIER_3_NEIGHBOURHOODS: Benalúa, Playa de San Juan

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_INCLUDE:
Alicante Centro (Plaza del Ayuntamiento, Explanada de España,
Cathedral area), Alicante El Barrio (Santa Cruz old town, narrow
streets below Castillo de Santa Bárbara), Alicante Ensanche
(Ensanche Diputación, El Postiguet beach area), Alicante El Puerto
(marina, Puerto deportivo), Benalúa, Carolinas Altas,
Carolinas Bajas, Playa de San Juan

GEOGRAPHIC_BOUNDARY_EXCLUDE:
Dénia, Calpe, Jávea, Altea, Benidorm, Villajoyosa, Elche,
Murcia, Valencia, or any location described as "outside Alicante
city", "province of Alicante", "Costa Blanca", "nearby town",
or another municipality in the region.
IMPORTANT: The Michelin-starred restaurants in Calpe (Audrey's,
Beat, Orobianco) and Dénia (Quique Dacosta 3★, Bon Amb 2★) are
in the province, not Alicante city — exclude these entirely.

LOCAL CONTEXT
════════════════════════════════════════

LOCAL_FOOD_VOCABULARY:
— arroz a banda (rice cooked in fish stock, the defining Alicante
  dish — traditionally two courses: rice first, then the fish)
— esgarraet (roasted red pepper and salt cod salad, intensely local)
— pericana (dried ñora pepper and salt cod condiment, rustic origin)
— caldero (fish and rice stew, regional Mar Menor variant)
— coca de mollitas (flatbread with pork lard crumbles, local staple)
— gamba roja (red prawn — closely associated with Dénia/Alicante
  region, used throughout local food writing as premium product)
— turrón de Alicante (hard nougat, DOP designation, Jijona origin)
— Monastrell (dominant red grape of Alicante DO wine region)
— La Terreta (informal term locals and food writers use for the
  Alicante region — signals local-register writing)
— El Barrio (the old town tapas zone below the castle — essential
  local shorthand for the historic restaurant district)

Use 2 Cross-6 vocabulary queries in Spanish targeting arroz a banda,
esgarraet, and La Terreta to catch local-register food writing that
generic city-wide queries miss.
