CITY RESEARCH NOTES
════════════════════════════════════════

Pamplona is the capital of Navarra, a Spanish community with a distinct
culinary identity shaped by its huerta (market garden), river trout, white
asparagus, piquillo peppers, and a pintxos culture related to but distinct
from the Basque Country.

KEY DISHES AND CULINARY VOCABULARY:
- Pintxos (local spelling — not pinchos): defining Pamplona eating format.
  Bar crawl (poteo) along Calle Estafeta, San Nicolás, Zapatería is the
  signature local experience.
- Espárragos de Navarra: white asparagus, regional trademark
- Pimientos del piquillo de Lodosa: roasted red peppers, often stuffed
- Pochas con chistorra: white beans with Navarran sausage
- Bacalao al ajoarriero: salt cod with garlic and piquillo
- Trucha a la navarra: trout with jamón
- Hongos / setas de temporada: seasonal wild mushrooms
- Verduras de la huerta: Navarran market garden vegetables
- Queso de oveja: sheep milk cheese
- Caza: game (woodcock, partridge, wild boar)
- Cuadrilla: group of friends eating together — core local social concept

BOTH-POOL ASSESSMENT:
Moderate probability. San Fermín / Running of the Bulls generates genuine
English-language food and travel writing beyond pure tourist content. Named
food writers have covered Pamplona for publications including Condé Nast
Traveller, Food & Wine, and Saveur. Expect 2–4 both-pool entries if pipeline
finds qualifying EN sources.

LANGUAGE NOTES:
