LOCAL CONTEXT
════════════════════════════════════════

LOCAL_FOOD_VOCABULARY:
— marinera (bread stick or toast topped with Russian salad and
  anchovy — the defining Murcian tapa, eaten in three bites)
— zarangollo (scrambled eggs with courgette and onion —
  quintessential Murcian home cooking, signals local writing)
— michirones (dried bean stew with ham bone and pork —
  traditional Murcian starter, found in most tapas bars)
— pastel de carne (meat pie with distinctive filo pastry lid —
  unique to Murcia, not found elsewhere in Spain)
— caldero / arroz caldero (fish and rice dish from Mar Menor
  fishermen — Murcia's answer to paella, DO NOT compare to paella
  in any extraction)
— huerta murciana (the fertile market garden around Murcia —
  the central concept of local food identity, used constantly
  in Murcian food writing)
— Calasparra rice (DOP rice grown in Murcia province — premium
  local product, signals serious local food writing)
— café asiático (coffee with condensed milk, brandy, Licor 43,
  cinnamon and lemon — Cartagena/Murcia signature drink)
— entierro (anchovies, crisps and olives — local tapa)
— Jumilla, Bullas, Yecla (local wine DOs — all in Murcia region)

Use 2 Cross-6 vocabulary queries in Spanish targeting marinera,
huerta murciana, and zarangollo to catch local-register food
writing that generic city-wide queries miss.
