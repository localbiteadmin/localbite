STRUCTURAL NOTES — TORONTO
────────────────────────────────────────────
1. EN-ONLY CITY: Toronto has no secondary language food writing pool.
   0 both-pool entries is structural, not a pipeline failure. All
   entries carry language_pool: "en".

2. THIN CRITICISM LANDSCAPE: Toronto's major newspaper critics are
   either behind paywalls (Toronto Star), have inactive food coverage
   (Globe and Mail), or have departed. The independent blogosphere is
   active but often anonymous. Only 4 sources confirmed in this run.

3. FOODISM TIER_C QUOTES: 19 of 20 Foodism entries have quotes under
   15 words (the article uses punchy short descriptions per its
   listicle format). These are assigned tier_c. The descriptions are
   confirmed from article fetches and are accurate — not fabricated.
   If the user wants to upgrade Foodism entries to tier_b, re-fetching
   the full article text for longer descriptions is recommended.

4. ADDRESSES WITH STREET ONLY: Some NOW Toronto entries have street
   names only (Sheppard Ave E, Danforth Ave, Milvan Dr, Morningside Ave,
   Bloor St W, Weston Rd, Portland St) without building numbers, as the
   article did not provide them. Geocoding may fail or return low-confidence
   results for these entries.

5. MULTIPLE-LOCATION RESTAURANTS: Midori Ramen (s4) and MSSM (s2) are
   listed as multiple-location restaurants. Address field is null;
   neighbourhood is "Multiple locations". Geocoding will fail — set
   geo_skip: true manually if desired.

6. AKIN EXCLUSION: Akin (51 Colborne St) was in the Foodism list but
   excluded after the pipeline's AI processing identified it as
   "Michelin-starred." Akin does NOT appear in the Part 1 MICHELIN_STARRED_EXCLUSIONS
   list. The identification came from the WebFetch summary, not necessarily
   from explicit article text. RECOMMEND: verify Akin's Michelin status
   before final deployment. If Akin is not starred, add it back to the
   dataset (Foodism source, 51 Colborne St, Financial District, tier_c).
